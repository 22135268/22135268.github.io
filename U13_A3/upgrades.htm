When should you upgrade your phone?

You should upgrade your phone when it becomes slow, the battery dies quickly, it stops receiving crucial security updates, or physical damage makes it difficult to use, often around the 2-4 year mark for most users, though some prefer longer if the device still performs well and gets updates. Key triggers include performance drops, full storage, crashing apps, lack of updates, or repair costs exceeding the phone's value.

Why should you upgrade your phone?

Upgrading your phone is necessary when you experience slow performance, degraded battery life, or a lack of security updates. Newer models provide improved cameras, better display quality, faster 5G connectivity, and increased storage capacity, which enhance daily usage. Upgrading also ensures your device remains compatible with the latest apps and secure against cyberthreats.

How often should you upgrade your phone?

Most consumers should upgrade their smartphone every 2 to 4 years, as this strikes a balance between performance, software support, and battery health.
