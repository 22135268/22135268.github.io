Types of phone services

Phone services are categorized into four main types: VoIP (internet-based), Mobile (cellular), Landline (analogue/digital), and specialized systems (PBX/Satellite). Modern, flexible, and cost-effective solutions like VoIP (e.g., hosted/cloud) are dominating the market, while traditional landlines remain in use for reliability. Mobile networks (3G-5G) offer high mobility, while PBX/KSU systems cater to corporate needs.

What are mobile phone providers

Phone carriers (or Mobile Network Operators/MNOs) provide cellular service via their own infrastructure, like Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile (US), or EE, O2, Vodafone, Three (UK); smaller companies (MVNOs) like Mint Mobile, Giffgaff, or Sky Mobile rent these networks to offer cheaper, often prepaid, plans, delivering the same coverage but sometimes with different perks or data limits.
